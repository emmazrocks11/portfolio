website redesign of

Skate Canada

October 2024 || Website UI Design

ROLE

Solo school project, UI Designer

TIMELINE

2 Weeks

SOFTWARE AND SKILLS

Figma, Branding and UI Design

01. Background

Recognized by the International Skating Union as Canada's official sanctioning body for figure skating, Skate Canada is the hub for those who want to learn, teach, watch and support the sport on ice. They manage elite national and international athletes while being the largest learn-to-skate teaching organization in Canada.

Project Overview

The challenge was to redesign a pre-existing website, tackling identified issues with a complete visual/structural overhaul. This involved a revamped sitemap and four hi-fidelity mockups (including the homepage) to address problems in usability, content, and visual design. As a former figure skater, I couldn't help but choose the website I used to visit so often.

Why I chose Skate Canada

To put it bluntly, I initially noticed a lot of weak UI elements upon my revisit (after a couple of years!?). The navigation bar covers nearly a third of the visual window, and the smaller text sizes don't compliment their chosen typeface. With further analysis, the layout and content of each individual page hinders its purpose for the user. Skate Canada is a large organization, so their website doesn't lack content overall. The main problem is that users cannot find the relevant information they need among the website with ease.

Weak UI Elements

Minimized visual range, low readability and legibility, intensely coloured backgrounds

Wrongly Placed Content

Missing About Us, repetitive buttons on a single page, peculiar emphasis on latest news, no focus on events

Poor Brand Application

Brand typeface and colours applied incorrectly, patriotic message limited to slogan

Selected pages: Learn to Skate, Athletes, and News (+ Home)
